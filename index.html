EARTHQUAKES NEAR YOU?

What are Earthquakes?

They are seismic waves that are caused by the sudden movement of materials within the Earth.

Events that can cause seismic waves are volcanic eruptions, explosions, landslides, avalanches, and even rushing rivers.


Where do Earthquakes Occur?

They occur along faults, which are fractures between blocks of rock. These fractures allow the blocks of rock to shift and move relative to one another. Faults are caused by the bumping and sliding of plates, which are much more common near the edges of them.

What to do DURING an earthquake?

DROP wherever you are onto your hands and knees.

COVER your head and neck with your arms. If you can crawl under a sturdy table or cover as shelter.

If there is no shelter in reach, crawl next to a main wall. STAY AWAY FROM WINDOWS!

Only crawl if you can reach better cover without exposing yourself.


HOLD onto the table or desk with one hand.

If not under a desk, hold onto your head and neck with both arms and hands.
